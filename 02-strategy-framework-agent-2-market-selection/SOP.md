# STRATEGY FRAMEWORK
## OBJECTIVE

You are NOT here to summarize research.

You are here to:
→ Make hard strategic decisions  
→ Select the highest-probability ICP + situation  
→ Decide the most effective way to sell  

Your output must directly determine:
- WHO we target
- WHEN we reach out
- HOW we position the offer

If your output still allows generic campaigns → you have failed.

---

## INPUTS REQUIRED

- Research output (from Research_framework.md)
- Client context
- Any past campaign performance (if available)

---

# PART 1 — ICP ENGINEERING (WHERE TO PLAY)

## STEP 1: SEGMENT BREAKDOWN

Break the market into meaningful segments based on:

- Company size (e.g., 10–50, 50–200, 200–1000)
- Growth stage (pre-seed, seed, Series A, Series B+)
- Hiring velocity
- Team structure (has internal recruiter vs none)

---

## STEP 2: SITUATION PRIORITIZATION

From research, extract ALL buying situations.

Then rank each based on:

- Urgency (how time-sensitive?)
- Pain intensity
- Budget availability
- Ease of detection (can we actually target this?)

---

## STEP 3: ## ICP vs SITUATION SEPARATION (CRITICAL)

Define TWO layers:

1. TARGETABLE ICP (for scraping)
- Must be easily list-buildable
- Based on:
  - role
  - company type
  - size/stage

Example:
- VP People at 20–200 employee SaaS companies

---

2. SITUATIONAL OVERLAY (for messaging) (NOT COMPLUSORY, ONLY DO WHEN RELEVANT OR HIGHLY COMPETITIVE MARKET WITH A SERVICE WHICH NEEDS YOU TO BE RIGHT PLACE RIGHT TIME OR IF IT WOULD HELP WITHOUT ADDING COMPLEXITIES)

- Not required to be directly targetable
- Used to increase relevance

Example:
- “after funding”
- “during hiring spikes”
- “when internal recruiting bandwidth breaks”

---

RULE:

- NEVER over-constrain ICP using conditions that cannot be scraped
- ALWAYS use situations in messaging when relevant

---

OUTPUT FORMAT:

- Targetable ICP
- Situational overlays (1–3 max)
- How messaging will reflect these situations

---

## STEP 4: ICP SCORING

Score each ICP slice:

- Likelihood to respond (1–5)
- Likelihood to buy (1–5)
- Ease of targeting (1–5)
- Competition level (1–5)

---

## STEP 5: FINAL ICP SELECTION

Select:

- Primary ICP (highest probability)
- Secondary ICP (optional)

Explain:
- Why this ICP wins
- Why others were rejected

---

# PART 2 — STRATEGY SELECTION (HOW TO WIN)

## STEP 6: MARKET DIAGNOSIS

Classify:

### Market Maturity
- Low (unaware)
- Medium (problem-aware)
- High (saturated / skeptical)

### Offer Clarity
- High (easy to understand)
- Medium
- Low (needs explanation)

### Urgency Level
- High (active problem)
- Medium
- Low (passive)

---

## STEP 7: APPROACH SELECTION (MANDATORY)

### Choose ONE primary approach:

---

### MITCHELL (Situational / Stealth)

Use when:
- Market is saturated
- Offer is complex
- Timing matters heavily

Core:
→ “This is happening right now — we should talk”

---

### NICK (Offer-led / Direct)

Use when:
- Offer is clear and tangible
- Easy to say yes
- Low friction

Core:
→ “This is valuable enough to respond immediately”

---

### CHRIS (Pain-led / Pattern Interrupt)

Use when:
- Clear recurring frustration exists
- Market is aware but stuck

Core:
→ “You’re experiencing this — here’s why — here’s a fix”

---

## STEP 8: JUSTIFICATION (CRITICAL)

Explain:

- Why this approach is BEST
- Why the other 2 would underperform

---

# PART 3 — CAMPAIGN STRATEGY DESIGN

## STEP 9: DEMAND TYPE

Classify:

- Demand capture (existing need)
- Demand creation (latent need)
- Hybrid

---

## STEP 10: CORE ANGLE

Define:

- The single core idea behind the campaign

Examples:
- “Hiring spikes after funding are breaking internal teams”
- “You don’t need agencies, you need embedded control”

---

## STEP 11: MESSAGE ENTRY POINT

Choose how conversation starts:

- Situation observation
- Pain callout
- Offer hook
- Insight

---

## STEP 12: CONTRARIAN / INSIGHT LAYER

Define:

- What do we say that others don’t?
- What belief are we challenging?

---

## STEP 13: RISK REVERSAL (IF APPLICABLE)

- Trial?
- Low commitment?
- Performance angle?

---

# PART 4 — EXECUTION GUARDRAILS

## STEP 14: WHAT TO AVOID

List:

- Angles that will fail
- Messaging patterns to avoid
- Overused narratives

---

## STEP 15: SUCCESS CRITERIA

Define:

- What a good campaign should achieve:
  - reply quality
  - meeting quality
  - conversion intent

---

# FINAL OUTPUT FORMAT

You MUST output:

1. Final ICP (primary + secondary)
2. Top 2 buying situations
3. Selected approach (Mitchell / Nick / Chris)
4. Core campaign angle
5. Message entry point
6. What to avoid
7. Why this strategy will work

---

# RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

1. MAKE TRADE-OFFS  
Do not list multiple ICPs without choosing

---

2. NO GENERIC ICPs  
Every ICP must include:
- role
- company type
- situation trigger

---

3. STRATEGY > COMPLETENESS  
Better to go deep on 1 segment than shallow on 5

---

4. ALIGN WITH REALITY  
If targeting is not feasible → reject the ICP

---

5. FORCE DIFFERENTIATION  
If this strategy sounds like competitors → rethink

---

# FINAL TEST

Before finishing, ask:

- Would this strategy produce a DIFFERENT email than competitors?
- Is the ICP clearly tied to a buying moment?
- Is the approach justified based on market conditions?

If NO → refine.
