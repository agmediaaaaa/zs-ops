# Offer Creation Module

Universal Offer Engineering and persona-specific offer frameworks for outbound / cold email.

---

## Universal Offer Engineering Prompt (Outbound / Cold Email)

### Role

You are a world-class B2B GTM strategist and direct response offer architect. Your expertise is converting weak deliverable-based services into strong, outcome-driven offers that generate demand in outbound channels (cold email, cold ads, outbound sales).

Your job is to transform deliverables into demand-generation offers that can be sold to cold audiences.

---

### Step 1 — Understand the Business

**Input the following:**

- **Business description:** [Describe the business]
- **Deliverables currently sold:** [List what they actually provide]

  Examples: Data lists, Recruiting services, Email infrastructure, Candidate sourcing, Lead generation, CRM setup, Consulting

- **Target customers:** [ICP description]

  Examples: SaaS founders, Recruitment agencies, Financial services firms, Manufacturing companies

---

### Step 2 — Diagnose the Offer

Analyze the deliverables and determine:

1. **Is this a demand capture offer or a demand generation offer?** Explain briefly.
2. **If demand capture,** identify the dependency that prevents results.

   Examples:
   - Data → requires cold email infrastructure
   - Leads → requires sales team
   - Candidates → requires hiring process
   - SEO → requires patience and content

3. **Identify the true desired outcome the buyer wants.**

   Examples: More qualified sales calls, Faster hiring, Higher placement revenue, Revenue growth, More pipeline, More booked demos

---

### Step 3 — Convert Deliverables Into Outcomes

Transform the deliverables into a clear, tangible result.

**Rules:**

- The offer must promise a **measurable outcome**
- The outcome must be **quantifiable**
- The offer must **remove dependencies**
- The buyer should **not need additional systems**

**Output format:**

- **Old deliverable:** e.g. "Email data lists"
- **Outcome transformation:** e.g. "Booked sales calls with qualified prospects"

---

### Step 4 — Remove Buyer Friction

Identify everything the buyer would normally need to succeed.

**Example — Cold email requires:** domains, inboxes, copywriting, deliverability, sending, list building, reply handling.

Then **bundle these elements** into the offer so the client only cares about the result.

**Output — Offer Stack Example**

| Instead of:     | Offer:                                                                 |
|-----------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Data lists      | Prospect data + Cold email infrastructure + Email copy + Sending system + Reply handling + Call booking |

---

### Step 5 — Build the Core Offer

Construct the offer using this structure.

**Offer Formula**

> We help [ICP] get [specific measurable result] in [timeframe] without [common pain].

**Example:**

> We help recruitment firms book 3–5 qualified job-order calls per month using outbound systems so founders don't have to run business development themselves.

---

### Step 6 — Add Proof and Risk Reversal

Add:

- **Quantification** (e.g. 3–5 job orders per month)
- **Proof** (e.g. 30+ ready-to-hire leads delivered)
- **Guarantee** (e.g. refund or performance guarantee)

---

### Step 7 — Final Offer Output

Produce:

1. **The Offer Statement** — Clear one-sentence outcome.
2. **Offer Breakdown** — List everything included.
3. **Why It Works** — Explain briefly why the offer is now demand generation instead of demand capture.
4. **Outbound Hook** — Create a cold email opener based on the outcome.

**Example hook:**

> "Quick question — if we put 3-5 hiring managers on your calendar next month looking for recruiters, would you even have room to take them?"

---

### Step 8 — Stress Test the Offer

Analyze:

- What assumptions might fail?
- What objections prospects will raise?
- What dependencies still exist?
- Suggest improvements.

---

### Example Input → Output

**Input**

- **Business:** Cold email data provider
- **Deliverables:** verified leads, email lists
- **ICP:** Recruitment agencies

**Output**

- **Offer:** We help recruitment firms book 3-5 qualified hiring manager calls per month using automated outbound systems.
- **Included:** Target account research, Prospect list building, inbox setup, cold email copy, sending system, reply handling, call booking.
- **Why it works:** The offer sells meetings, not data.

---

### Critical Principles the Model Must Follow

- Buyers purchase **results**, not deliverables.
- **Demand generation** offers outperform demand capture offers in outbound.
- **Remove all dependencies** required for success.
- The offer must have **clear economic value**.
- The result must be **measurable**.

---

## The 5-Layer Outbound Offer Framework

Strong outbound offers follow this structure (stack persuasion layers).

### Layer 1 — The Economic Outcome

The result that affects revenue.

| Bad example              | Good example                              |
|--------------------------|-------------------------------------------|
| "AI outbound system"     | "3–5 qualified hiring manager calls per month" |

**Examples across industries:**

| Industry    | Weak               | Strong      |
|------------|--------------------|-------------|
| Recruiting | candidate sourcing | job orders  |
| SaaS       | leads              | demos       |
| Agencies   | traffic            | clients     |
| Consulting | advice             | revenue increase |

**Rule:** Always tie the offer to revenue or growth.

---

### Layer 2 — The Mechanism

Cold prospects ask: *"Why should I believe this works?"* You need a **unique mechanism**.

- **Bad:** "Cold email system"
- **Better:** "AI-driven outbound targeting companies currently hiring"

Mechanisms create believability (e.g. intent signals, proprietary datasets, automation systems, candidate networks, AI personalization).

---

### Layer 3 — Friction Removal

Most buyers think: *"Sounds good but I don't have time."* The offer must **remove effort**.

- **Instead of:** "We help with outbound"
- **Say:** "We run the entire outbound engine so you don't have to do BD."

Remove: learning, setup, time, management.

---

### Layer 4 — Proof Density

Cold traffic believes proof, not claims. Add: numbers, case studies, volume metrics, client examples.

- **Instead of:** "We generate leads"
- **Say:** "Delivered 30+ ready-to-hire leads to a $200M firm."

---

### Layer 5 — Risk Reversal

Cold prospects think: *"What if this doesn't work?"* Reduce risk.

Examples: performance guarantee, pilot program, cancel anytime, replacement guarantee.

---

### The Offer Stack Formula

> We help [ICP] get [economic outcome]  
> Using [unique mechanism]  
> Without [major friction]  
> Backed by [proof]  
> With [risk reversal]

**Example — Weak vs Strong**

- **Weak:** AI outbound system for recruitment firms.
- **Strong:** We help recruitment firms book 3–5 hiring manager calls per month using an AI outbound system targeting companies actively hiring, so founders don't have to run BD themselves. We've already generated 30+ ready-to-hire leads for a $200M recruiting firm. And if you don't see traction in 60 days, you don't pay.

---

### Reality Check — Pipeline vs Meetings

For recruitment firm founders, the real pain is often **pipeline inconsistency** (some months 0 job orders, some months 5). The strongest offer might be **pipeline stability**, not meetings.

**Example framing:** Predictable job order pipeline every month.

**Hard question:** Can you actually guarantee job orders? Probably not. Decide whether you sell **meetings** or **pipeline**. The wrong outcome promise is the #1 reason outbound offers collapse.

## Persona-Specific Offers

The offer cannot exist in isolation from the persona. A generic “staffing offer” is meaningless because hiring decisions are contextual to the buyer’s moment in the company lifecycle.

**Process:** Persona → Moment → Fear/Aspiration → Offer

---

### 1 — AI SaaS Founder (10–15 Employees)

| Their reality | |
|---------------|--|
| **Pain** | Founder still running sales or product hiring; no hiring infrastructure; one bad hire could cost months of runway |
| **Aspiration** | Build a founding team; hire the first revenue leaders |
| **Fear** | Hiring someone who "looks good on paper but can't build from zero." |

**Offer:**

> Many AI founders around the 10–15 employee stage are still closing deals themselves. We help them hire their first founding AE or GTM leader so the founder can step out of sales. If you're considering that hire this year, would it be crazy to test us on just that one role?

**Why it works:** Speaks to founder time; ties hiring to revenue leverage.

---

### 2 — SaaS Founder After Seed / Series A

| Their reality | |
|---------------|--|
| **Pain** | Investor pressure to grow; need to build leadership bench |
| **Fear** | Hiring too junior or too senior |

**Offer:**

> After raising a round, most SaaS founders need to build their first real leadership team. We help companies at that stage hire founding GTM and product leaders who've already scaled a startup before. Curious — is leadership hiring something you're tackling this year?

---

### 3 — Head of Talent at a 200–500 Person Tech Company

| Their reality | |
|---------------|--|
| **Pain** | Overwhelmed hiring pipeline; pressure from multiple departments |
| **Fear** | Hiring delays impacting growth |

**Offer:**

> When hiring ramps up, internal talent teams usually get buried in sourcing. We step in on the hard-to-source roles so your team can stay focused on closing candidates. If there's one role that's been sitting open for a while, we could start there.

---

### 4 — PE / Portfolio Company CEO

| Their reality | |
|---------------|--|
| **Pain** | Aggressive growth targets; need proven operators |
| **Fear** | Hiring executives who cannot scale |

**Offer:**

> Portfolio companies often need leaders who've already scaled companies at your stage. We specialize in headhunting operators who have already built and scaled teams in similar companies. If there's one leadership hire you're evaluating this quarter, we could start there.

---

## The Bridge Principle

The offer should bridge: **Current State → Desired State**.

**Example for the AI founder:**

- **Current state:** Founder still doing sales.
- **Desired state:** Founder focuses on product and strategy.
- **Bridge:** Hire a founding AE.

The recruiting service becomes the vehicle to cross that bridge.

---

## Offer Creation Framework (Checklist)

When designing persona-specific staffing offers:

| Field | Use it for |
|-------|------------|
| **Persona** | Who is the buyer? |
| **Company stage** | Where are they? |
| **Their current problem** | What’s blocking them? |
| **Their aspiration** | What do they want? |
| **Their biggest hiring fear** | What are they afraid of? |
| **Bridge** | What role unlocks that next stage? |
| **Offer** | Position recruiting as the fastest path to that transition. |

---

## Important Challenge

Even with correct persona targeting, one assumption can fail: **You may assume they already believe hiring is the solution.**

Many founders think: *"I'll just do it myself for now."* / *"We're not ready to hire."*

So the offer must show the **consequence of not hiring**.

**Example:** Founder doing sales → growth bottleneck.
