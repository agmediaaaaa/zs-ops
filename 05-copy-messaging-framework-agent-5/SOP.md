
## OBJECTIVE

You are NOT writing a copy.

You are:
→ Translating how the ICP already thinks into words  
→ Making the message feel instantly recognizable  
→ Making the offer feel like a natural next step  

If it feels like:
- marketing  
- explanation  
- “good copywriting”  

→ YOU HAVE FAILED

---

## INPUTS REQUIRED

- Offer Framework Output (MANDATORY)
- Strategy Output (ICP + approach)
- Research Output (beliefs, frustrations, behaviors)

---

# PART 0 — HARD RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

1. SIMPLE > SMART  
If it sounds clever → rewrite  

2. CLEAR > PERSUASIVE  
If it needs effort to understand → fails  

3. THEIR LANGUAGE > YOUR LANGUAGE  
No internal jargon  

4. SPECIFIC > BROAD  
Broad = ignored  

5. ONE IDEA PER MESSAGE  
No stacking  

6. WORLDVIEW > EVERYTHING  
If it doesn’t match how they think → discard  

---

# PART 1 — WORLDVIEW ENGINE (CORE)

## STEP 1: INTERNAL MONOLOGUE SIMULATION (MANDATORY)

Before writing ANY copy:

Write 5–10 raw thoughts the ICP has in their head.

Rules:
- messy
- unstructured
- no jargon
- no “business language”

---

### Example (SaaS Founder 11–50 employees)

- “i’ll just handle hiring myself for now”
- “we’ll hire when we need to”
- “why is it so hard to find good people”
- “feels like we keep missing strong candidates”
- “i don’t want to hire a recruiter full-time yet”
- “this is taking way more time than it should”

---

## STEP 2: DO NOT TRANSLATE — REUSE

BAD:
→ “inconsistent hiring process leads to talent loss”

GOOD:
→ “we only start hiring when something breaks  
   and by then the good candidates are gone”

---

## STEP 3: BEHAVIOR > THEORY

Focus on:
- what they DO
- what keeps happening

NOT:
- why it happens (unless reframing)

---

## STEP 4: IMPERFECT LANGUAGE ONLY

If it sounds:
- polished
- structured
- like a LinkedIn post  

→ rewrite

---

# PART 2 — AWARENESS ADAPTATION

## STEP 5: IDENTIFY AWARENESS LEVEL

---

### LEVEL 1: UNAWARE

- doesn’t see clear problem  

Use:
→ observation  
→ pattern  

---

### LEVEL 2: PROBLEM AWARE

- feels frustration  

Use:
→ mirror their thoughts  
→ describe repeated issues  

---

### LEVEL 3: SOLUTION AWARE

- knows options exist  

Use:
→ better approach  
→ contrast with current  

---

### LEVEL 4: PRODUCT AWARE

- skeptical  

Use:
→ proof  
→ specifics  
→ remove risk  

---

## STEP 6: MATCH OR FAIL

If awareness ≠ message  
→ copy underperforms regardless of quality  

---

# PART 3 — MESSAGE CONSTRUCTION

## DEFAULT RULE

Message =

1. Their thought  
2. Their reality  
3. Natural solution (offer)  
4. Low-friction CTA  

---

## STRUCTURE 1 — NATURAL (PRIMARY)

Use when:
→ strong worldview clarity  

---

Format:

- internal thought
- consequence
- soft solution
- CTA

---

Example:

“you probably end up jumping into hiring yourself  
and it slows everything else down  

and by the time you start, the good candidates are already gone  

some teams just bring someone in to run hiring properly for a bit  
and switch it off when they don’t need it  

relevant right now?”

---

---

## STRUCTURE 2 — SITUATIONAL (OPTIONAL)

Use when:
→ real trigger exists  

---

Format:

- observation
- implication
- natural solution

---

---

## STRUCTURE 3 — DIRECT (SIMPLIFIED)

Use when:
→ clarity > nuance  

---

Format:

- outcome
- grounded proof
- CTA

---

---

## STRUCTURE 4 — REFRAME (ADVANCED)

Use when:
→ ICP stuck in wrong belief  

---

Format:

- problem
- why it happens
- better way

---

---

# PART 4 — WORLDVIEW-ALIGNED OFFER INSERTION

## STEP 7: OFFER MUST FEEL LIKE THEIR IDEA

BAD:
→ “we provide flexible recruiting support”

GOOD:
→ “some teams just bring someone in for a bit  
   and switch it off when they don’t need it”

---

## STEP 8: REMOVE STRUCTURE FROM OFFER

If it sounds like:
- pricing model  
- delivery model  

→ rewrite into:
- behavior
- scenario

---

---

# PART 5 — BELIEVABILITY ENGINE

## STEP 9: REAL > IMPRESSIVE

Replace:

❌ “we deliver fast results”  
✅ “roles that sat open for months closed in weeks”

---

## STEP 10: LIMIT CLAIM INTENSITY

If it feels:
- too perfect  
- too certain  

→ reduce it  

---

---

# PART 6 — RELEVANCE (WITHOUT SIGNALS)

## STEP 11: DEFAULT TO WORLDVIEW

Even WITHOUT:

- hiring signals  
- funding  
- triggers  

Message should work via:

→ shared behavior  
→ shared frustration  

---

## STEP 12: CONTEXT (ONLY IF REAL)

Use only when verified:

- hiring push  
- recruiter role open  
- growth phase  

---

## STEP 13: NO FAKE PERSONALIZATION

Instant delete:

- “noticed your growth”  
- “impressed by your journey”  

---

---

# PART 7 — TENSION (SUBTLE)

## STEP 14: SHOW WHAT’S BREAKING

Example:

→ “hiring only starts when something breaks”  
→ “roles sit open while everything else moves”  

---

NOT:

- aggressive  
- dramatic  

---

---

# PART 8 — CTA DESIGN

## STEP 15: LOW-FRICTION ONLY

Use:

- “relevant right now?”  
- “worth exploring?”  
- “is this something you're dealing with?”  

---

Avoid:

- “book a call”  
- “schedule time”  

---

---

# PART 9 — VARIATION ENGINE

## STEP 16: GENERATE 5–10 VARIANTS

Each must:

- use different thought patterns  
- NOT reword same message  

---

Types:

- behavior-led  
- consequence-led  
- proof-led  
- minimal/direct  
- tension-led  

---

---

# PART 10 — GENERICITY FILTER (KILL SWITCH)

Reject if:

- could apply to multiple ICPs  
- sounds like marketing  
- uses buzzwords  
- feels “written”  

---

---

# PART 11 — FINAL TEST

Ask:

1. Does this sound like their internal thoughts?
2. Can it be understood instantly?
3. Does it feel like their CURRENT reality?
4. Would they ignore this?

If ANY doubt → rewrite  

---

# GOLD PRINCIPLE

Great copy doesn’t persuade.

It reflects.

The moment the ICP thinks:

→ “this is exactly what’s happening”

You’ve already won.
