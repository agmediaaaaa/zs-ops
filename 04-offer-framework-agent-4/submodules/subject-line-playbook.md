# Playbook: High-Volume Cold Email Subject Line Strategy

## 1) Core Philosophy
- Do not sell in the subject line; the only job is to earn the open.
- Make the subject look like an internal email from a colleague.
- Curiosity beats clarity when paired with a truthful first-line bridge.

## 2) Top-Performing Subject Line Categories

### A) Ultra-short (1-2 words)
- quick question
- question
- intro
- feedback
- idea

Why this works:
- Feels informal, fast, and human.
- Avoids "campaign template" patterns.

### B) Relevant hook (contextual)
- `[Company Name] + [Your Company Name]`
- thoughts on `[specific project or post]`
- re: `[relevant topic]` (use cautiously; avoid deceptive use)

Why this works:
- Creates immediate relevance without pitching.

### C) Non-standard pattern interrupt
- hello
- checking in
- lost?
- reaching out

Why this works:
- Breaks expected B2B format and can increase opens.

## 3) Implementation Rules
1. Use sentence case or lowercase; avoid title case.
2. No clickbait. Subject must connect naturally to line one.
3. Do not over-personalize subject lines at scale if it lowers trust.
4. Keep subjects short and easy to parse on mobile.

## 4) Testing Framework (A/B/C)
Run 3 subject variants per campaign segment:
- Test A (safe): `quick question`
- Test B (contextual): `[Company Name] growth`
- Test C (pattern interrupt): `thoughts?`

Primary metric:
- Open rate target: 50-60%+.
- If below threshold, inspect deliverability first, then subject fit.

Secondary checks:
- Positive reply rate by subject bucket
- Spam complaint trends
- Domain-level performance differences

## 5) Subject Line Hall of Fame
- question for `[First Name]`
- partnership?
- growth at `[Company]`
- video for `[First Name]`

## 6) Fit with Agent 4 Offer Work
Use this submodule after the offer is finalized:
1. Pick one "safe" subject matched to offer type.
2. Pick one context-led subject tied to the ICP situation.
3. Pick one pattern-interrupt subject.
4. Ensure the first sentence logically matches each subject variant.
5. Launch and score by segment; keep winners and retire weak variants.
